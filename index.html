Overdoses in the United States

Most stories about drug overdoses in the US center around a specific narrative, particularly that West Virginia is the worst in the nation for ODs. With more than 60 ODs per 100,000 people, they lead the nation by that metric. But is that really the best way to rank OD deaths in America? And is there a happy story anywhere in the country?

This story breaks down Center for Disease Control data from three states and asks what exactly these numbers mean and how that translates into reality. Is West Virginia really bearing the brunt of the opioid epidemic?
